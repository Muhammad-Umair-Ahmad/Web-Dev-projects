Ice Cream
  1. Whip the cream till it is fluffy.
  2. Cook cocoa powder in milk till mixed completely into a smooth consistency.
  3. Add Nestlé MILKMAID, cooked cocoa powder, vanilla essence, whipped cream & mix well.
  4. Pour into a freezing tray and freeze till half set. Remove and beat with a whisk or electric beater until smooth. Doing so will help keep the texture of the ice cream soft and creamy while it sets.
  5. Pour back into tray and freeze till well set.

Bilyani
  1. Wash and soak 2 cups of basmati rice in water for 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, boil water with salt, 2-3 cloves, 1 bay leaf, and a cinnamon stick. Add the soaked rice and cook until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee and sauté 2 sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnish.
  4. Add 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste to the pan and cook for 1 minute until aromatic.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft. Mix in 1/2 cup yogurt, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
  6. Add marinated chicken (or mutton) to the masala and cook until the meat is tender and oil separates.
  7. Layer partially cooked rice over the meat mixture. Sprinkle the fried onions, fresh coriander, and mint leaves on top.
  8. Drizzle saffron milk (a few saffron strands soaked in warm milk) and 1 tbsp ghee over the rice for flavor and aroma.
  9. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal with dough. Cook on low heat (dum) for 20–25 minutes.
  10. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving. Gently mix layers and serve hot with raita or salad.

Dahi Pallay
  1. Soak 1 cup urad dal (or moong dal) in water for 4–5 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain and grind the dal to a smooth batter using minimal water. Add salt and 1/2 tsp baking soda; mix well.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry spoonfuls of the batter until golden and puffed. Drain on paper towels.
  4. Soak the fried ballay in warm water for 15–20 minutes to soften them.
  5. Gently squeeze the soaked ballay to remove excess water and place them in a serving dish.
  6. In a bowl, whisk 2–3 cups of yogurt with a pinch of salt and sugar until smooth.
  7. Pour the whisked yogurt generously over the softened ballay.
  8. Sprinkle roasted cumin powder, red chili powder, chaat masala, and black salt to taste.
  9. Drizzle tamarind chutney and mint chutney over the top for extra flavor.
  10. Garnish with chopped fresh coriander and optionally some crispy papri or sev before serving.
